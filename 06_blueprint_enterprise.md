# Design System Strategy: The Sovereign Ledger

## 1. Overview & Creative North Star
**Creative North Star: The Sovereign Ledger**
In the world of enterprise banking, "trust" is often mistranslated into "rigidity." This design system rejects the clunky, line-heavy aesthetic of legacy financial tools in favor of a **High-End Editorial** approach. We are building a "Sovereign Ledger"—an environment that feels authoritative and immutable, yet possesses the fluid intelligence of modern AI. 

We break the "template" look by prioritizing **Tonal Depth** over structural outlines. By using a structured grid with intentional asymmetry in data visualization and overlapping AI modules, we create a workspace that feels like a premium digital terminal rather than a basic web app.

---

## 2. Colors & Surface Architecture
The palette is rooted in a sophisticated grayscale, punctuated by an authoritative banking blue (`primary`) and a visionary AI indigo (`tertiary`).

### The "No-Line" Rule
Standard 1px solid borders are prohibited for defining sections. Boundaries must be defined through **Background Tonal Shifts**. For example, a navigation sidebar should use `surface-container-low` to sit behind a `surface` main content area. This creates a "seam" rather than a "cut," making the UI feel like a singular, integrated piece of software.

### Surface Hierarchy & Nesting
Treat the UI as a series of physical layers. Use the following hierarchy to create "nested" importance:
1.  **Base Layer:** `surface` (The foundation).
2.  **Sectional Layer:** `surface-container-low` (Sidebars, secondary panels).
3.  **Active Workspace:** `surface-container-lowest` (The "white" of the page/main cards).
4.  **Interactive/Pop-over:** `surface-container-high` (Modals or floating AI insights).

### The Glass & Gradient Rule
To distinguish "Human-Input" data from "AI-Generated" insights:
*   **AI Accents:** Use semi-transparent `tertiary-container` with a `backdrop-blur` for AI-generated tooltips or panels.
*   **Signature Textures:** Apply a subtle linear gradient from `primary` to `primary-dim` on main action buttons to provide a "machined" professional finish.

---

## 3. Typography: Precision Hierarchy
We utilize **Inter** for its neutral, technical clarity. In a high-density environment, typography is your primary navigation tool.

*   **The Display Scale:** Use `display-sm` for high-level portfolio totals. It should feel like a headline in a financial journal—bold and unapologetic.
*   **The Data Workhorse:** `body-md` is the standard for data entry. However, use `label-md` and `label-sm` (all-caps with 0.05em tracking) for table headers and metadata to provide an "institutional" feel.
*   **AI Insights:** Any text generated by AI should use `title-sm` with a slightly tighter line-height and be paired with the `tertiary` color to signal a change in the "voice" of the data.

---

## 4. Elevation & Depth
Depth is achieved through **Tonal Layering**, not shadows.

*   **The Layering Principle:** Place a `surface-container-lowest` card on a `surface-container` background. The subtle 2-3% shift in lightness creates a natural lift that is easier on the eyes than a drop shadow.
*   **Ambient Shadows:** If a floating state is required (e.g., a context menu), use a shadow with a 24px blur, 0% spread, and 4% opacity using the `on-surface` color. It should feel like a soft glow, not a dark silhouette.
*   **The "Ghost Border" Fallback:** If a border is required for high-density data accessibility, use the `outline-variant` token at **15% opacity**. This creates a "hint" of a container without cluttering the visual field.
*   **Glassmorphism:** Use `surface-container-lowest` at 80% opacity with a `12px blur` for AI-generated overlays. This ensures the user never loses context of the underlying "Sovereign" data.

## 5. Components

### Cards & Data Lists
*   **Style:** Forbid the use of divider lines. 
*   **Separation:** Use `8px` or `16px` of vertical white space or a subtle switch to `surface-container-low` on hover to separate items.
*   **AI Indicators:** An AI-generated row in a table should have a `2px` vertical "accent line" of `tertiary` on its far left edge, rather than a full-box border.

### Buttons
*   **Primary:** `primary` background with `on-primary` text. Use `md` (0.375rem) roundedness for a modern, architectural feel.
*   **Secondary:** No background. Use a `ghost-border` (outline-variant @ 20%) with `primary` text.
*   **AI Action:** Use a subtle gradient: `tertiary` to `primary`.

### Dense Data Tables
*   **Header:** `surface-container-highest` background, `label-sm` typography.
*   **Rows:** Alternating `surface-container-lowest` and `surface` backgrounds. No horizontal lines. 
*   **Alignment:** Numbers must be tabular-lining (monospaced) for vertical scanning.

### Status Badges
*   **Execution:** Small, pill-shaped (`full` roundedness). 
*   **Coloring:** Use `error-container` with `on-error-container` for alerts, but keep the saturation low to maintain the professional grayscale aesthetic.

---

## 6. Do’s and Don'ts

### Do:
*   **Do** use "Optical Alignment." Sometimes a table header needs to be offset by 1px to visually align with the data below.
*   **Do** use `tertiary` (AI purple) only for items that are *dynamically* changing or generated.
*   **Do** maximize "negative space" within dense tables by using the `body-sm` font size with a generous `1.5x` line-height.

### Don't:
*   **Don't** use 100% black (#000000) for text. Always use `on-surface` to maintain a professional, soft-contrast "ink on paper" feel.
*   **Don't** use standard shadows. If a component feels flat, increase the background contrast between it and its parent container.
*   **Don't** use icons without labels in the sidebar. In banking, clarity is more important than "minimalism."
